How the prep works

Content and strategy, built around your weak spots.

The MCAT rewards students who understand systems, not students who memorize lists. Every plan starts by diagnosing where you are, then targets the highest-yield gaps, in the sciences, in CARS reasoning, and in pacing, so your study time produces real point gains.

What we focus on

  • Diagnosing your strongest and weakest sections from practice data
  • High-yield content review across bio/biochem, chem/phys, and psych/soc
  • CARS reasoning habits and a repeatable approach to passages
  • Timing, stamina, and a section-by-section pacing plan
  • Reviewing full-lengths so every practice test becomes points

Who this is for

  • Premed students preparing for an upcoming test date
  • Retakers who need a sharper, more strategic plan
  • Self-studiers who want expert help on specific subjects
  • Students who feel overwhelmed and want structure and calm
  • High achievers aiming to push a strong score even higher

Common questions

MCAT prep, answered.

Which MCAT package should I choose?

It depends on your timeline and how much you plan to self-study. Shorter packages suit students who mainly need strategy and targeted help on weak areas; longer packages suit students who want comprehensive, paced coverage of every section. The free consultation is the fastest way to match a package to your goals.

Are sessions online or in person?

Both. Sessions are available online for students anywhere, and in person for students in the Madison, Wisconsin area.

How long is each session?

Every session in every package is 90 minutes, long enough for focused content review, practice, and strategy without losing momentum.

Can you help with a retake?

Absolutely. Retakers often benefit most from a fresh, strategic plan built around their previous score breakdown and the specific sections holding them back.
